Aloo Paratha


Description

Aloo Paratha is a classic North Indian stuffed flatbread made with whole wheat dough and a spicy, mashed potato filling. It’s warm, hearty, and full of flavor — perfect for breakfast or a comforting meal. Served with butter, curd, or pickle, it’s one of the most loved Indian dishes.

Ingredients

    For the dough

  • 2 cups whole wheat flour
  • Water (as needed for kneading)
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tsp oil (optional)

    • For the stuffing

  • 3 medium potatoes (boiled & mashed)
  • 1–2 green chilies (finely chopped)
  • ½ tsp red chili powder
  • ½ tsp turmeric
  • ½ tsp garam masala
  • ½ tsp cumin powder
  • 1 tbsp chopped coriander
  • Salt to taste

Steps

  1. In a bowl, mix wheat flour, salt, and water to knead a soft dough. Rest for 10 minutes.
  2. Mix mashed potatoes with green chilies, spices, coriander, and salt to prepare the stuffing.
  3. Divide the dough into medium-sized balls.
  4. Roll one ball lightly, place stuffing in the center, and close the edges.
  5. Roll again gently into a flat paratha.
  6. Heat a tawa and cook the paratha on both sides with a little oil or ghee until golden brown.
  7. Serve hot with butter, curd, or pickle.
